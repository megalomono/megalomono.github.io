About me

I'm currently working as software engineer for Health In Code in A Coruña (Spain). In my spare time I dedicate myself to tinker on my own projects to learn new technologies, I practice martial arts and I drink beer.

How do I work

As the saying goes, "When in Rome, do as Romans do" , so in general, I adapt to the ways of work and possibilities of the environment. However, I have my quirks and preferences, as every programmer ;-)

  • Testing

    I'm not dogmatic about it, but in general writing tests forces you to think and that's good. Writing the test first is useful if you don't know where to start.

  • Versioning

    Once you get used to working with multiple branches there is no turning back. Make a merge in Subversion is a real pain in the ass, so I generally prefer to use Git.

  • Simplicity

    Usually things are complicated enough by themselves without anyone touching them, so I try to keep the code as simple as possible.

  • Automation

    If I can have a machine doing my work for me, I'll have it. The other option is to have an intern, but they usually demand decent working conditions.

  • Agility

    The processes are a mean and not an end. Functional documents of 200 pages are like pseudo-erotic novels: there are people who make a living by writing them, but are worthless.

Links of interest

Some useful links:

  • Robert Martin's blog (Uncle Bob): If you've never read one of his posts or seen any of his videos, you should. You're going to laugh.
  • Joel On Software: The Joel Spolsky blog. Though he has stopped now writing to focus on other things, most of his articles are timeless.
  • Code Of Honor: Have you ever wonder why the protoss dragoons take so long to find the right way? Here's the answer.

Recommended books

If you are a software developer (sorry) or a wannabe developer (bad idea), you should read these books:

  • Design Patterns, by Gamma, Helm, Johnson y Vlissides.
  • Refactoring, by Martin Fowler
  • Clean Code, by Robert C. Martin

And if you're going to develop complex systems, read this too:

  • Domain Driven Design, by Eric Evans.
